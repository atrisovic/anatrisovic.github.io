About

I am a Research Scientist at the FutureTech Lab at MIT. My research interests include measuring the impact of technology and AI on science and society, applications of graph neural networks in social sciences, high-dimensional datasets and benchmarks, reproducibility and replicability of modern research methods. Previously, I was a Research Associate in the National Studies on Air Pollution and Health (NSAPH) group, at the Department of Biostatistics of Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health and the Institute for Quantitative Social Science of Harvard's Faculty of Arts and Sciences. I worked on the Harvard Data Commons and the Dataverse project, among other projects. I was a postdoctoral fellow at the University of Chicago in 2019, where I worked with the Energy Policy Institute (EPIC) and the Library. I completed my Ph.D. in 2018 at the University of Cambridge and CERN, where I worked on the LHCb experiment, CERN Open Data and CERN Analysis Preservation.
